Regex

"Wildcards on steriods"

August 29, 2015

One blog post I read described regular expressions, or regex, as wildcards on steroids. They are highly applicable in search or find and replace situations. They work by defining a pattern for a string to use when processing text. Regex is a powerful concept and is incorporated in many programming languages, including our very own dear Ruby.

One of the things that makes regex look confusing is it uses a lot of symbols. Some of them have special meaning - for example, a dollar sign ($) matches at the end of a string, and \b signifies a word boundary. They're scattered throughout the literal characters that we read as normal language, making things look confusing, but if you understand their meaning, you can decipher the code.

In Ruby, regular expressions are objects, just like strings and classes. They have their own methods and operators.
